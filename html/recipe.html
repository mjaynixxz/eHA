Baked Moi Moi Recipe
Ingredients List of Steps List of details
For 12 muffin cups of Moi Moi, you will need:
  • 350g black eyed beans
  • 1 habanero pepper
  • 1 big red bell pepper
  • 2 stock cubes
  • 2 cooking spoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 onion
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Warm water (for grinding and mixing)
  1. If you are using beans seeds, peel the beans, blend and mix the Nigerian Moi Moi as usual using the following procedures:
  2. Set your oven to 200°C, 390F or Gas Mark 6 to preheat. Set it to bake that is, top and down heating.
  3. Rub vegetable oil in the muffin cups.
  4. Pour the Moi Moi mix into the muffin cups.
  5. Cover with aluminium foil and fold the foil into the sides of the muffin cup. This aluminium foil prevents the Moi Moi from drying up.
  6. Pour some water in the oven tray and place the muffin tray with the Moi Moi mix in the tray.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes. Check it from time to time to ensure there is always water in the oven tray. If it looks like it is drying up, top up the waterbundleRenderer.renderToStream
  8. When done, leave to cool down completely and bring them out from the muffin cups.

Serve immediately or store in the freezer. Nigerian Moi Moi stores well in the freezer. Serve with Peppered Fish.




Pepper Rice Stew Recipe
Ingredients List of Steps List of Details
  • 4 big tatashe peppers
  • 4 Habanero Peppers (or to your taste)
  • 4 fresh tomatoes
  • 5 cooking spoons sunflower oil (vegetable oil)
  • 1 red onion
  • 250g shaki (cow tripe)
  • 250g ponmo (cow skin)
  • Fish (I used Gilt Head Sea bream fish in the video below)
  • 2 small seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 tablespoon Nigerian curry powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  1. Boil the shaki and ponmo with the thyme, seasoning cubes and some of the diced onion till they are soft. These meats take a while to cook so if you have a pressure pot, use it when cooking them.
  2. Pour the vegetable oil into a clean dry pot and heat it up.
  3. Once very hot, add the remaining onions and fry till it turns brown.
  4. Add the tomato and pepper blend and fry till the perfect fried tomato consistency is achieved. Click here for how to fry the best tomato stew.
  5. Add salt, curry powder and the boiled meats.
  6. Cover and continue to cook till it steams up again and the pepper rice stew is ready.
  7. Clean and cut the fish then rub salt on them.
  8. Deepfry in vegetable oil.

Tatashe is a hot and tapered Nigerian pepper. You can use sweet peppers or fresh paprika which are not spicy hot as alternative. If you do not have shaki and ponmo, use any part of beef available to you.




Vegetable Soup Recipe
Ingredients List of Steps List of Details
  • 2 kg frozen spinach
  • 250g lamb’s lettuce
  • 2kg Assorted meat
    • ribs
    • cow skin (ponmo)
    • cow tripe (shaki)
  • Stock fish
  • 4 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 6 small Knorr cubes
  • Sweet pepper (for colour)
  • Habanero pepper (to your taste)
  • 2 big bulbs of onion
  • 1½ cups of red palm oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  1. Cook the meats and stock fish with the seasoning cubes and half of the chopped onions.
  2. When done, remove all the meat and fish and set aside.
  3. De-bone and separate the stockfish into small pieces.
  4. Make sure the stock is as little as possible, if not, boil it down first before continuing.Set the stock on the stove, add palm oil, crayfish, the remaining chopped onions. Cover and cok for about 7 minutes to allow the palm oil integrate into the soup.
  5. Add the peppers, the lambs lettuce, the meats and fish.
  6. Cover and once it boils again, add the spinach.
  7. Transfer the soup to cool containers immediately so the vegetables do not change colour.

The spinach acts as an alternative for Ugu. The lamb’s lettuce is a close alternative to water leaves. You can reduce the quantity of palm oil but spinach has a very strong taste so you need enough palm oil to mask that taste.
